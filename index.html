What is mining?

Mining is the process of adding transaction records to cryptocurrencies public ledger of past transactions. It is the process that keeps the cryptocurrency network alive.

Mining is intentionally designed to be resource-intensive and difficult so that the number of blocks found each day by miners remains steady. This so called “proof-of-work” concept is what gives legitimacy to the ledger contained in the network. This is why it’s done with either specialized hardware or GPUs - miners.



Where is the catch?

Mining is also used to introduce currency into the system: Miners are paid any transaction fees as well as a "subsidy" of newly created coins. This serves as motivating people to provide security for the system.

Mining Pool

To minimize the stagnation of profit, miners are added to so called “mining pools”, which is collaboration of multiple miners who mine the same block in order to increase their chances of processing a profitable block. Depending on the size of the pool, overall profit is split between miners. We are implementing our own mining pool in order to increase both effectiveness and profitability of pools.

Auto-switching mining

Our data scientists are working on machine learning algorithms for gathering and predicting the coin values and automatically switching miners to work on the most profitable ones. With a volatile market, this projects requires careful attention before being put in place.
